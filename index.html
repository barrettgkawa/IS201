St Louis Cardinals

A Small Overview of the Team

Fun Facts

  • The Cardinals have the second most World Series titles in the MLB with 11.
  • The team was originally founded in 1881 as the St Louis Browns before being renamed the Perfectos and then as the Cardinals in 1900.
  • Despite being in the smallest metropolitan in the league, the Cardinals average attendance is second in the league with around 40,000 people attending each game.
  • The Cardinals play in Busch Stadium III which was originally opened in 2006. They won their tenth World Series there that year!

Players To Know

Pitchers

  • Sonny Gray - 2023 AL Cy Young runner up
  • Ryan Helsley - 2022 All-Star, Second Team All-MLB

Fielders

  • Paul Goldschmidt - 2022 NL MVP
  • Nolan Arenado - 10x Gold Glove, 6x Platinum Glove, 5x Silver Slugger
  • Willson Contreras - 3x All-Star, 2016 World Series Champion
  • Lars Nootbaar - 2023 World Baseball Classic Champion, International Phenom

Season Forecast

The Cardinals open the 2024 season fresh off the worst season they have had in 30 years. The season came as quite a surprise for the reigning NL Central champions, but a combination of injuries, lack of defense, and blown saves led the Cardinals to an unprecedented last place finish.

Having revamped the pitching rotation over the off season, the Cardinals are poised to once again be a threat in the NL Central division. The mixture of seasoned veterans and exciting young players should make their lineup dangerous on any given night. The questions remain with the team on whether the rotation bolstering will be enough. Further questions include will the young players and rookies step up while we also wonder if the older players can give us a few more good years. If all goes well, it is very likely the 2024 can end with the Cardinals back in the playoffs or even raising banner number 12.
